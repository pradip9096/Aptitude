Quick Reference

\(n^3 = n \times n \times n\)

\(10^3 = 1000\)

\(11^3 = 1331\)

\(12^3 = 1728\)

‚ö° Cube Exam Cheat Sheet

1. The Snapshot Definition

  • Concept: Multiplying a number by itself twice (\(n \times n \times n\)).
  • The Sign Rule: Cubes keep the sign of the base.
    • \((+)\) Base \(\rightarrow (+)\) Result
    • \((-)\) Base \(\rightarrow (-)\) Result
    • Example: \((-2)^3 = -8\).
  • The "Flip" Relationship:
    • If a number ends in 2, its cube ends in 8 (and vice versa).
    • If a number ends in 3, its cube ends in 7 (and vice versa).
    • All other digits (\(0, 1, 4, 5, 6, 9\)) keep the same ending digit.

\(12^3 = 1728\)

Ratio Method (For 2-digit numbers):

  • Technique: For a number \(ab\) (where ratio \(r = b/a\)):
  • Row: \(a^3 \quad a^2b \quad ab^2 \quad b^3\)
  • Double Middles: Double the two middle terms.
  • Add: Sum the columns (carrying over digits).

Example: \(12^3\) (\(a=1, b=2\))

Terms: \(1 \quad 2 \quad 4 \quad 8\)
Double: \(-- \quad 4 \quad 8 \quad --\)
Sum: \(1 \quad 6 \quad 12 \quad 8\)
Carry: \(1 \quad 7 \quad 2 \quad 8 \rightarrow \mathbf{1728}\).

Nearby Base Approximation:

For numbers close to 10 or 100, use \((N+d)^3 \approx N^3 + 3N^2d\).

Example: \(101^3 \approx 1,000,000 + 3(10,000)(1) = 1,030,000\).

3. MCQ Elimination Tactics (Verification)

Strategy: Cubes grow extremely fast. Use range checks to kill options.

1. The Unit Digit Check:

  • Question ends in 2 ‚Üí Answer ends in 8.
  • Question ends in 3 ‚Üí Answer ends in 7.
  • Question ends in 7 ‚Üí Answer ends in 3.
  • Question ends in 8 ‚Üí Answer ends in 2.
  • All others match (\(4 \rightarrow 4, 6 \rightarrow 6\)).

2. Digital Root Check:

  • Calculate sum of digits of the base (\(D\)).
  • Cube that sum (\(D^3\)).
  • The digital root of the answer must match the digital root of \(D^3\).

Example: \(13^3 = 2197\)

Base Sum: \(1+3=4\). Cube: \(4^3=64 \rightarrow 6+4=10 \rightarrow \mathbf{1}\).
Answer Sum: \(2+1+9+7 = 19 \rightarrow 1+9=10 \rightarrow \mathbf{1}\). (Match).

3. Range Estimation:

Task: \(24^3\)

Benchmarks: \(20^3 = 8,000\) and \(30^3 = 27,000\).
Analysis: 24 is less than half way. Answer is likely around 12,000‚Äì15,000. Eliminate options like 9,000 (too small) or 25,000 (too big).

4. Essential Formulas

Concept Formula
Sum of Cubes \(a^3 + b^3 = (a+b)(a^2 - ab + b^2)\)
Difference of Cubes \(a^3 - b^3 = (a-b)(a^2 + ab + b^2)\)
Cube of Sum \((a+b)^3 = a^3 + b^3 + 3ab(a+b)\)
Cube of Difference \((a-b)^3 = a^3 - b^3 - 3ab(a-b)\)
Sum of First n Cubes \(\left( \frac{n(n+1)}{2} \right)^2\)

5. Word Problem "Trigger Words"

If you see these phrases, you likely need to cube a number:

  • "Find the Volume of a cube/box..."
  • "Find the Capacity of a tank..."
  • "Weight scales by..." (Square-Cube Law context)
  • "Compound Interest for 3 years..." (Involves \(r^3\)).

6. The "Exam Trap" Alert ‚ö†Ô∏è

Trap 1: Ignoring the Negative.
  • Problem: \((-5)^3 = ?\)
  • Trap: 125.
  • Fix: The cube of a negative is Negative (-125).
Trap 2: Decimal Shift.
  • Problem: \(0.2^3 = ?\)
  • Trap: 0.8 or 0.08.
  • Fix: Cubing triples the decimal places. 1 place ‚Üí 3 places. Answer: 0.008.
Trap 3: Confusing Formulas.
  • \(a^3 + b^3 \ne (a+b)^3\).
  • Example: \(1^3 + 2^3 = 9\). \((1+2)^3 = 27\). They are totally different.
