‚ö° HCF Exam Cheat Sheet

1. The Snapshot Definition

  • Concept: The largest number that divides all numbers in a set exactly.
  • The Boundary Rule: \(HCF \le \text{Smallest Number in the Set}\).
    • Implication: If an option is larger than the smallest number in the question, eliminate it immediately.
  • The Co-Prime Rule: If numbers share no common factors, HCF = 1.

2. Speed-Math Shortcuts (The "Difference" Method)

This is the most powerful shortcut for HCF.

  • The Logic: The HCF of two numbers is always a factor of their difference.
  • The Steps:
    1. Find the difference between the two numbers.
    2. Check if this difference divides both numbers.
    3. If yes, the difference is the HCF.
    4. If no, check the factors of that difference (starting from the largest).

Example: Find HCF of \(48\) and \(60\)

‚Ä¢ Difference: \(60 - 48 = 12\).
‚Ä¢ Does \(12\) divide \(48\) and \(60\)? Yes.
‚Ä¢ HCF = 12.

3. MCQ Elimination Tactics (The "Eye-Test")

Strategy: Do not calculate from scratch. Test the Options.

1. The "Largest Winner":

Since we want the Highest Common Factor, start testing from the largest option provided.

  • Step 1: Pick the biggest option.
  • Step 2: Check if it divides all numbers in the question.
  • Step 3: If yes, mark it. If no, move to the next biggest option.

2. The Odd-Number Hack:

  • If any number in the set is Odd, the HCF must be Odd.
  • Action: Eliminate all even options instantly.

3. The Zero Hack:

  • If numbers end in \(0\), the HCF usually ends in \(0\) or \(5\).

4. Essential Formulas

Concept Formula
Two Numbers Relationship \(HCF(a, b) = \frac{a \times b}{LCM(a, b)}\)
HCF of Fractions \(HCF = \frac{\text{HCF of Numerators}}{\text{LCM of Denominators}}\)
HCF of Exponents Take the Lowest Power of common prime bases.
Remainder Theorem If asking for the largest number that divides \(A, B, C\) leaving remainders \(x, y, z\):
Find HCF of \((A-x), (B-y), (C-z)\).

5. Word Problem "Trigger Words"

If you see these phrases, you need to calculate HCF:

  • "Find the maximum..."
  • "Find the greatest size..."
  • "Cut into equal pieces..."
  • "Largest square tiles to cover a floor..."
  • "Maximum capacity of a container..."

6. The "Exam Trap" Alert ‚ö†Ô∏è

Trap 1: Confusing HCF with LCM
  • Fix: Remember HCF = Huge divisor (but small number compared to LCM).
Trap 2: Forgetting the Difference Rule limits
  • Fix: The difference itself isn't always the HCF; sometimes it is a factor of the difference. (e.g., HCF of 10 and 15 is 5, but the difference is 5. HCF of 10 and 24 is 2, but the difference is 14. 2 is a factor of 14).
Trap 3: "Remainder" Confusion
  • Fix: Always subtract the remainders from the numbers before finding the HCF.
