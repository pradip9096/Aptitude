‚ö° LCM Exam Cheat Sheet

1. The Snapshot Definition

  • Concept: The smallest positive number that is divisible by all numbers in a set.
  • The Boundary Rule: \(LCM \ge \text{Largest Number in the Set}\).
    • Implication: If an option is smaller than the largest number in the question, eliminate it immediately.
  • The Zero Rule: If any number in the set is 0, the LCM is 0.

2. Speed-Math Shortcuts (No Pen Required)

The Absorption Rule:

  • Technique: If a smaller number is a factor of a larger number, ignore the smaller number completely.
Example: LCM of \(5, 10, 20\). Since 5 and 10 divide into 20, the LCM is 20.

The Multiplier Method:

  • Technique: Take the largest number in the set and check its multiples (\(\times 2, \times 3, \times 4 \dots\)) until you find one divisible by the other numbers.
Example: LCM of \(10\) and \(12\). Largest is 12.
‚Ä¢ \(12 \times 1 = 12\) (No)
‚Ä¢ \(12 \times 2 = 24\) (No)
‚Ä¢ \(12 \times 3 = 36\) (No)
‚Ä¢ \(12 \times 4 = 48\) (No)
‚Ä¢ \(12 \times 5 = 60\) (Yes! Divisible by 10). LCM = 60.

The Prime-Time Rule:

  • Technique: If all numbers are prime, the LCM is their Product.
Example: LCM of \(3, 5, 7\) is \(3 \times 5 \times 7 = 105\).

3. MCQ Elimination Tactics (The "Eye-Test")

Strategy: Do not calculate. Test the Options.

1. Divisibility Check:

The correct option must be divisible by every number in the question.

  • Usage: Use Divisibility Rules (\(3, 4, 9, 10\)) to kill wrong options.
Example: Question asks for LCM of \(9\) and \(14\). Option 80 is wrong because \(8+0=8\) (not divisible by 9).

2. The Smallest Winner:

If multiple options are divisible by all numbers, pick the smallest one.

3. Last Digit Hack:

  • If numbers end in \(0\) and \(5\), LCM ends in \(0\).
  • If numbers are even, LCM is even.

LCM of Exponents Take the Highest Power of every prime base present.

5. Word Problem "Trigger Words"

If you see these phrases in a word problem, you need to calculate LCM:

  • "Find the minimum..."
  • "Find the smallest..."
  • "When will they meet again?"
  • "Events occurring simultaneously..."
  • "Traffic lights changing together..."

6. The "Exam Trap" Alert ‚ö†Ô∏è

Trap 1: Confusing HCF and LCM
  • Fix: Remember LCM = Large result (Multiples).
Trap 2: Picking the first common multiple you see
  • Fix: Check if there is a smaller option that also works.
Trap 3: Ignoring units
  • Fix: Ensure all time/distance units are the same before calculating (e.g., convert all to seconds).
