‚ö° Multiplication Exam Cheat Sheet

1. The Snapshot Definition

  • Concept: Repeated addition or scaling a value.
  • The Sign Rule:
    • \((+) \times (+) = (+)\)
    • \((-) \times (-) = (+)\)
    • \((+) \times (-) = (-)\)
  • The Identity Rule: Any number \(\times 1\) stays the same.
  • The Zero Rule: Any number \(\times 0\) becomes \(0\).

2. Speed-Math Shortcuts (Mental Math)

The "Double and Halve" Trick:

  • Technique: If multiplying a large even number by a number ending in 5 (or a fraction), double one side and halve the other. The product remains the same but becomes easier to solve.
Example: \(16 \times 2.5 \rightarrow\) (Half of 16) \(\times\) (Double 2.5) \(\rightarrow 8 \times 5 = \mathbf{40}\).
Example: \(14 \times 15 \rightarrow 7 \times 30 = \mathbf{210}\).

The "Split and Merge" (Distributive Law):

  • Technique: Break difficult numbers into friendly parts (10s, 5s, 1s), multiply separately, and add.
Example: \(13 \times 12 \rightarrow 13 \times (10 + 2) \rightarrow 130 + 26 = \mathbf{156}\).

The Rule of 11:

  • Technique: To multiply a two-digit number by 11, separate the digits and place their sum in the middle.
Example: \(32 \times 11\). Separate 3 and 2. Sum is 5. Result: 352.
Exception: If the sum is \(>9\), carry the 1 over. (\(48 \times 11 \rightarrow 4 \dots (12) \dots 8 \rightarrow 528\)).

Multiply by 5, 50, 500:

  • Technique: Multiply by 10 (add a zero) and divide by 2.
Example: \(48 \times 5 \rightarrow 480 \div 2 = \mathbf{240}\).

3. MCQ Elimination Tactics (Verification)

Strategy: Don't compute the full number if you don't have to.

1. The Unit Digit Method (Last Digit Check):

The last digit of the product depends only on the last digits of the factors.

Example: \(12\mathbf{3} \times 45\mathbf{6}\). Last digit calculation: \(3 \times 6 = 18\). The answer must end in 8. Eliminate options ending in 0, 2, 4, etc.

2. Estimation (Range Check):

Round the numbers to the nearest 10 or 100 to find the approximate range.

Example: \(49 \times 102\)
Estimate: \(50 \times 100 = 5000\)
Action: If one option is 500 and another is 50,000, eliminate them immediately.

3. Digital Root (Sum of Digits):

Sum the digits of the factors, multiply them, and check if it matches the sum of digits of the answer.

Example: \(12 \times 13 = 156\)
Check: \((1+2) \times (1+3) = 3 \times 4 = 12 \rightarrow (1+2) = \mathbf{3}\)
Answer: \(1+5+6 = 12 \rightarrow \mathbf{3}\). (Match confirmed).

4. Essential Formulas & Series

Concept Formula
Difference of Squares \((a+b)(a-b) = a^2 - b^2\)
Use for: \(19 \times 21 \rightarrow (20-1)(20+1) = 400 - 1 = 399\).
Squaring (End in 5) For number \(n5\): Result starts with \(n \times (n+1)\) and ends in \(25\).
Example: \(35^2 \rightarrow 3 \times 4 = 12 \dots\) End with \(25 \rightarrow \mathbf{1225}\).
Square (Sum) \((a+b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2\)
Square (Diff) \((a-b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2\)

5. Word Problem "Trigger Words"

If you see these phrases, you need to multiply:

  • "Product of..."
  • "Times as many..."
  • "Area of a rectangle..."
  • "Total cost of \(x\) items at \(y\) price..."
  • "Of" (when used with fractions/percentages, e.g., "50% of 20").

6. The "Exam Trap" Alert ‚ö†Ô∏è

Trap 1: Decimal Misplacement
  • Fix: Count total decimal places in the problem and ensure the answer has the same number. (\(0.2 \times 0.3\) has two total decimal places \(\rightarrow 0.06\), not \(0.6\)).
Trap 2: Ignoring Units
  • Fix: Multiplying "meters" by "meters" creates "square meters." Ensure the units match the options.
Trap 3: Negative Sign Errors
  • Fix: Count the negative signs. Even number of signs = Positive result. Odd number of signs = Negative result.
