‚ö° Square Root Exam Cheat Sheet

1. The Snapshot Definition

  • Concept: Finding the "side length" of a square area.
  • The Symbol: \(\sqrt{x}\) always refers to the positive (principal) root.
  • Domain: For real numbers, the radicand (number inside) must be non-negative (\(\ge 0\)).
  • Inequality Rule: If \(x > 1\), then \(\sqrt{x} < x\). If \(0 < x < 1\), then \(\sqrt{x} > x\).
    • Example: \(\sqrt{0.25} = 0.5\) (The root is bigger than the original number).

2. Speed-Math Shortcuts (Approximation)

The "Linear Approximation" Formula:

Use this to find the root of a non-perfect square quickly.

Formula: \(\sqrt{N} \approx \sqrt{P} + \frac{N - P}{2 \times \sqrt{P}}\)

Where: \(N\) is your number, and \(P\) is the closest perfect square.

Example: Find \(\sqrt{50}\)

‚Ä¢ Closest square \(P = 49\) (Root is 7).
‚Ä¢ \(\sqrt{50} \approx 7 + \frac{50 - 49}{2 \times 7} = 7 + \frac{1}{14} \approx \mathbf{7.07}\).

Prime Factorization (Simplifying Roots):

Technique: "Jailbreak" pairs of numbers. For every pair inside, one escapes outside.

Example: \(\sqrt{72}\)

‚Ä¢ \(72 = 36 \times 2 = (6 \times 6) \times 2\).
‚Ä¢ The pair of 6s escapes ‚Üí \(6\sqrt{2}\).

Memorize these Roots (The "Big 3"):

  • \(\sqrt{2} \approx 1.414\)
  • \(\sqrt{3} \approx 1.732\)
  • \(\sqrt{5} \approx 2.236\)

3. MCQ Elimination Tactics (Verification)

Strategy: It is usually faster to square the options than to root the question.

1. The "Unit Digit" Reverse Check:

Look at the last digit of the Question (\(Q\)) and the Option (\(O\)).

  • If \(Q\) ends in 1, \(O\) must end in 1 or 9.
  • If \(Q\) ends in 4, \(O\) must end in 2 or 8.
  • If \(Q\) ends in 5, \(O\) must end in 5.
  • If \(Q\) ends in 6, \(O\) must end in 4 or 6.
  • If \(Q\) ends in 9, \(O\) must end in 3 or 7.

Example: Which is \(\sqrt{5329}\)? Options: A) 72, B) 73, C) 77, D) 78

Check: Question ends in 9. Option must end in 3 or 7. Eliminate A and D.

2. Range Estimation:

Task: \(\sqrt{1500}\)

Benchmarks: \(30^2 = 900\), \(40^2 = 1600\).
Analysis: 1500 is close to 1600, so the answer must be close to 40 (e.g., 38 or 39). Eliminate options like 32 or 45.

3. Digital Root Check:

  • Calculate digital root of the Question.
  • Calculate digital root of the Option, then square it.
  • They must match.

4. Essential Formulas & Rules

Concept Rule/Formula
Product Rule \(\sqrt{a \times b} = \sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{b}\)
Quotient Rule \(\sqrt{\frac{a}{b}} = \frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}}\)
Root of Exponent \(\sqrt{x^n} = x^{n/2}\)
Example: \(\sqrt{10^6} = 10^3 = 1000\).
Nested Roots \(\sqrt{x\sqrt{x\sqrt{x\dots}}} = x\)

5. Word Problem "Trigger Words"

If you see these phrases, you likely need a square root:

  • "Find the length of the side of a square..."
  • "Find the diagonal..." (Pythagoras: \(c = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}\))
  • "Find the radius of a circle with Area \(A\)..." (\(r = \sqrt{A / \pi}\))
  • "Time it takes to fall..." (Physics: \(t = \sqrt{2d/g}\))

6. The "Exam Trap" Alert ‚ö†Ô∏è

Trap 1: The "Split Sum" Error.
  • Mistake: \(\sqrt{16 + 9} = 4 + 3 = 7\).
  • Fix: Add first! \(\sqrt{25} = 5\). Never split a root across a \(+\) or \(-\).
Trap 2: Decimal Roots.
  • Mistake: \(\sqrt{0.9} = 0.3\).
  • Fix: \(0.3 \times 0.3 = 0.09\), not \(0.9\). Actually, \(\sqrt{0.9} \approx 0.95\).
Trap 3: Confusing Units.
  • Fix: If Area is in \(cm^2\), the root (side length) is in \(cm\).
